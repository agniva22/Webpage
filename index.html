About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. scholar at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research Bhopal, India, where I am deeply engaged in exploring the intersection of learning and control for autonomous vehicles. My research focuses on advancing autonomous navigation in multi-agent systems by leveraging cutting-edge techniques in data-driven reinforcement learning and model predictive control (MPC).

Specifically, I am working on developing deep reinforcement learning algorithms that enable autonomous vehicles to make well-ordered control decisions in complex, dynamic environments involving multiple agents. My work addresses critical challenges such as managing static and dynamic obstacles, optimizing real-time decision-making, and enhancing the accuracy, flexibility, and safety of autonomous systems in real-world scenarios.

I am passionate about pushing the boundaries of intelligent systems to create more reliable, adaptive, and safe autonomous vehicles for the future of mobility solutions.
